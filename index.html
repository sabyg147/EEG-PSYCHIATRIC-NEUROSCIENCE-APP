Behind the Model

Feature Engineering

We extract powerful clinical features from raw EEG channels:

FAA (Frontal Alpha Asymmetry): Difference in alpha power between left/right hemispheres. Negative FAA strongly links to depression.
Theta/Alpha Ratio: Indicator of cognitive fatigue common in mood disorders.

XGBoost & Ensembles

Our pipeline uses a tuned XGBoost Classifier achieving 80.82% binary accuracy (Healthy vs Mood Disorder). Extreme Gradient Boosting builds sequential decision trees to minimize prediction error natively handling sparse matrixes.

Architecture Pipeline

Raw CSV ➔ Drop Metadata ➔ Impute Medians ➔ Extract FAA/Ratios ➔ StandardScaler ➔ VarianceThreshold ➔ XGBoost ➔ Softmax Probability

Clinical Research & Predictions

NeuroScan's prediction framework is fundamentally rooted in established psychiatric EEG literature. Research demonstrates that patients with Mood Disorders exhibit consistent deviations in Alpha, Beta, and Theta rhythms across the frontal and parietal lobes.

The Alpha Asymmetry Hypothesis

Frontal Alpha Asymmetry (FAA) is the golden standard biomarker for analyzing depressive behaviors. Greater relative right frontal activity (negative FAA) is consistently associated with withdrawal-oriented negative affect, anxiety, and clinical depression.

Theta/Alpha Ratio

Cognitive fatigue—a core symptom of mood disorders—manifests as elevated Theta power and reduced Alpha power. Our models weigh the Theta/Alpha ratio remarkably high, detecting early-stage cognitive decline linked to major depressive episodes.
